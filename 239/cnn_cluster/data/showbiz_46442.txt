Portman also won Golden Globe and SAG best actor trophies in recent weeks.When &quot;King's Speech&quot; director Tom Hooper accepted the best director Oscar, he noted that it was his mother who found the script for him.&quot;The moral of this story is listen to your mother,&quot; Hooper said.&quot;King's Speech&quot; writer David Seidler pointed out that at 73, he was the oldest person to ever win best original screenplay.&quot;My father always said to me I would be a late bloomer,&quot; Seidler said, noting that at 73 he is the oldest winner ever in the category. &quot;I hope that record is broken quickly and often.&quot;Colin Firth, who portrayed the stuttering King George VI in &quot;The King's Speech,&quot; completed his sweep of best actor honors with the Oscar win.&quot;I have a feeling my career has just peaked,&quot; said Firth, who also won a Golden Globe and the SAG award.Christian Bale repeated his Golden Globe and SAG award wins by taking the best supporting actor Oscar for his role in &quot;The Fighter.&quot;Melissa Leo, who played the mother and manager of two boxers in &quot;The Fighter,&quot; completed her trifecta of major awards when 94-year-old Kirk Douglas handed her the best supporting actress Oscar. Leo also won Golden Globe and SAG best actress trophies.Douglas, who walked with a cane and spoke slowly with slurred speech, managed to upstage Leo with his humor, jokingly delaying reading what was in the envelope.
